The Music of Neuromatch

Neuromatch

Neuromatch Academy is a phenomenal organisation which in 2021 ran two summer schools a computational neuroscience summer school and a deep learning summer school. I’ve had been very grateful to be a volunter at the first iteration of Neuromatch Academy in 2020 as part of the Student Recruitment group when we reviewed 3000 applications and as a content reviewer. In 2021 once again I was part of the Student Recruitment group where we reviewed over 5000 applications and as a content creator, which was I was told would "not be much work", it was but it was also a high point of my teaching year and 20 year career.

You can read about the inception and running of the Neuromatch Academcy summer school in this very interesting and informative blog by Gunnar Blohm. Or you can read about the dramatic last minute ban of Iranian students and TAs in 2020 due to some US sanctions and then even more dramatic laster minute lift of the ban . Or you can read the paper about the goal teaching philosophy goal of Neuromatch to make it global accessibility. My blog is about none of that it is about the link between music, learning and Neuromatch or to be more specific why I made playlists for the summer schools.

Why Link Music and Learning

When I had to make the move to teaching online I felt that the start of every lecture was very abrupt and to be honest I felt awkward as the only person with their camera on. Normally with in person lectures I would begin the lecture by chatting to the class as students came in about different things, this would help the students relax and encourage engagement. This was not possible in an online setting as for many different reasons most students did/could not turn on their cameras or microphones. I wanted to find a way to reduce both my and their awkwardness and encourage engagement, which brought me to the decision to start every class with a song that would have some link to the lecture. The songs acted as a sound check for the students and as an ice-breaker and change of pace for the everyone.

I found the game of picking songs that would have some relevance to the materials both fun and challenging, the songs ranged from classical to pop to rock to jazz. For example, I picked the excellent “Zombie” by Fela Kuti to motivate a lecture on predator-prey models, and I was enjoying the song so much that I started dad dancing along with my camera on, which I hope made everyone embarressed but also feel a but more at ease.

I choose to play Zombie by Fela Kuti at the start of the predator prey lecture which was pretty cool so I half-chair danced along because it is a great song, which was embarrassing for everyone.

The most kudos I got for my song choice was when I played “The Boys In The Better Land” by Fontaines DC, it was not relevant to the lecture but the band had just been nominated for a Grammy. Overall, the student reaction was positive, maybe not about all the song choices, but they responded to the change of pace and it generated some chat and added variety to the day, now to Neuromatch playlists.

The Neuromatch Playlists

As Neuromatch Academy is a massive online summer school with students almost 5000 students from over 100 countries split between the computational neuroscience summer school and the deep learning summer school. Fining a link with the the other members of neuromatch can seem daunting. So, I decided to make two playlist one for each summer school with the lofty goals:
  • hopefully helping some students to connect to each other and the materials,
  • to add some levity to the learning experience,
  • as a little thank you to all the people involved in making the materials,
  • to encourage me to look at all the materials.
The songs and their links to the materials were posted on twitter and on the summer schools discord music channels. The playlists generated a bit of discussion from the students and volunteers which was lovely and it made me read the materials.

Computational Neuroscience Playlist

Computational Neuroscience Neuromatch started with a Python refresher which I paired it with “Holst: The Planets”. In particular the song “Jupyter: the Bringer of Joy”, which in my mind is the unofficial anthem of Neuromatch as I believe Neuromatch is the Bringer of Joy to many students.
